Childhood

I never had a choice other than living in the future. Escaping childhood has always been my greatest utopia. The nostalgia, smelly memories, loud schools and firey stares, combined, just rot my soul.

The further away I got from this, the happier I got. Still, whenever I have reencounters, through a former school colleague, or similar situation, I immediately feel entrapped by its life-sucking claws.

And the issue is that there's childhood everywhere, in all ages.

Today, it's the ruling ideology.

Slaves and masters of their own ignorance and lethargy.

Kids, with their egocentric megalomanic fake emotions, delusions of authenticity, crowding together, little gangs, poisoning souls and rivers with indolence and feeble insurgency.
Self-deluded one might change the world. Too hungry, blind and imbecile for reasoning or empathy.

And yet adults, with their cold, formal and competitive mechanics, often dead, are just simple to ignore, and then easy to assemble. Just mind your business.

Perhaps, I will always keep a child inside, the one I never got to live, but I'm glad to see the years passing by.

It may all be a fast rollercoaster, but it is one pointing up.

A sinoid with a positive slope.

I guess I was right. If I was made out of water,as time passes, metaphorically, I'd be scotch, not sparkling water.
