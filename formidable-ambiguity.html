Formidable ambiguity

Such an unusual sentence.
Two subjects, one verb.
The most formidable of ambiguities.
I look into your eyes
And I just say it:
"God, I love you".

Yes,
You, the true father.
You, the omnipresent possibility.
You, the artesan of our fates.
You, the tracks of our train.
You, the composer of our songs.
You, the river in our hearts.
God, I love you.

And, yes,
You, the spider I have no fears.
You, the living chores of my soul.
You, the tear I get to wipe.
You, the only music I care to sing.
You, the true eyes I can't escape.
You, the one guardian of my heart.
God, I love you.
