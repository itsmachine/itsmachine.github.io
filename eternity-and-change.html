Eternity and change

I must admit: Nature made me suitable for loops, routines, iterations.

A mind that was mechanically engineered.

Eternity, in everything, has always been my goal.

Change has always been my enemy.

God, Metaphysics, Tradition, they apparently just made it all easier for myself.

But I was wrong.

God may be eternal, the only one actually, but this is yet another special trait only he has.

If a bird just tries to find eternity himself, he may as well just go insane.

God's point is not to avoid change.

God's point is to embrace change, fill it with light and send it to the one righteous path there is.

God's point is to experience the new colors, tastes, angles, and sounds - and let that refine you.

How could you not?

To become eternal is not to be a fearful algorithm, trying to carefully replicate what's happened, what's known.

To become eternal is simply to be part of God's purpose itself.

To become eternal is simply to see, hear, feel, think, hope, love and be.
