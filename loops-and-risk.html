Loops and risk

I dislike two thoughts of the contemporary men: loops and risk.
For the former, the world is kind of a curse, or a blessing, depending or where you're born, or where your luck is. They always take a step back, complacent and quiet with whatever happens, as if there would be no other outcome.
For the latter, the world is kind of jungle, and everything, especially the bad, could happen at any time, so why even bother? They don't live life, they hedge and mostly avoid any risk, hand-pick the few and tasteless actions they do. Too weak and afraid of a puddle, like a pension fund picks the few and unmovable stocks.
For me, I've never believed in them.
For me, I believe in will.
I believe in passion.
I believe in strength.
I believe in love.
There is no loop, or curse, to be followed no matter what. No matter the blessing, no matter the curse, it is the will of strong and formidable hearts that take the future to an unimaginably bright and magnificent place.
There is no risk, no probability, no statical analysis. There is hard work, talent, intelligence, love, altruism, sweat and patience, using our hands, foot, souls, hearts, so that any path can be traced, any castle can be built, any story can be written.
It is from 0 to 1, and everything can be done.
