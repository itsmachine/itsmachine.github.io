On giving

The greatest gift that God gives is not one of materiality, such as inheritance, luck, opportunities.

The greatest gift is, instead, the one gift of being.

Because, every person is package with pros and cons and, if one genuinely listens to one self, one could set itself free from all oppressions, lies, slavements.

Even so, no one is perfect and, as such, only find true harmony to be when the complementary soul enters into its path.

Is there a better definition of Love? Perhaps, but this is what I have today.

And what do I always wanted as a lover?

What could I have done?

For myself, I think true Love doesn't mean simply "giving".

Giving gifts, poems, hugs, kisses, cars, travels, money, or anything else.

Love truly is the building of a Truth and utopia that two hearts find to be the ideal.

I would never want to give you anything.

I would never want you to give me anything.

You are not seller, neither am I buyer.

Because we build together.

That's what I believe and always wanted.

I've seen too many cases of princesses locked in a castle of luxury, superficiality, gold, exorbitance and irrediamebly existential sadness.

I never wanted to just give you.

That's too easy and it's not the job of a lover.

The job of a lover is to set the other free.

And, as such, if I gave anything, it would be my future.

The future that I'd be willing to share only with you.

To be written not by my hands, but with our hands.

Not with my desires, but ours.

The castle I imagined, it doesn't matter how big or wealthy it is.

What matters is that this castle would be just as mine as yours.

Fruit of our work.

Fruit of our beliefs.

Fruit of our truths.

Fruit of our desires.

Fruit of our beings.

If I wasn't as able to build recently

To put bricks together

Is that

I can't do that with one hand

I may have the muscle

But sometimes

All I needed was the hand to motivate my ways.
